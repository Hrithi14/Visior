Research Title

Multi-Site Cardiovascular Risk Stratification Using Federated Machine Learning on Longitudinal Patient Cohorts

Abstract

This study aims to develop and validate a federated machine learning model for cardiovascular risk stratification across multiple hospital sites. By leveraging anonymized longitudinal patient data from the CARDIO-PRECISION-II trial, the research seeks to improve early detection of major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE) by an estimated 23% compared to traditional risk scoring methods.

Methodology

Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Data harmonization and federated learning infrastructure setup across 5 participating institutions.
Phase 2 (Months 4-8): Model training using federated averaging with differential privacy guarantees.
Phase 3 (Months 9-12): Validation against held-out test sets and bias assessment across demographic subgroups.
Phase 4 (Months 13-14): Manuscript preparation and public release of de-identified aggregate statistics.
